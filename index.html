What clogged gutters and damaged shingles can do to your home.

Small roofline problems often start quietly. Regular maintenance helps reduce water exposure and gives homeowners a chance to fix minor issues before they spread.

Check My Home
💧

Clogged gutters can redirect water where it does not belong.

When gutters cannot drain correctly, rainwater can spill over the edge, back up near the roofline, or collect around the base of the home.

  • 1Foundation moisture: Overflow can pool near the foundation and contribute to settling, cracks, or basement dampness.
  • 2Fascia and soffit damage: Trapped water can soak roofline wood, leading to rot, staining, and pest-friendly soft spots.
  • 3Siding and landscape damage: Constant spillover can stain siding, wash out mulch, and damage plants or walkways below.
  • 4Winter ice problems: Blocked drainage can make freezing conditions worse and add stress along the gutter line.
🏠

Broken or missing shingles can expose the roof system.

Shingles are your roof’s first line of defense. A small damaged area can let wind-driven rain reach materials that are not meant to stay wet.

  • 1Roof leaks: Water can enter beneath lifted, cracked, or missing shingles and show up as ceiling stains.
  • 2Decking deterioration: Repeated moisture can weaken the wood sheathing under the shingles.
  • 3Insulation and attic issues: Water intrusion can reduce insulation performance and create damp attic conditions.
  • 4Higher repair costs: Addressing a small shingle issue early is often simpler than waiting for interior water damage.
